> If you are finding things hard at university (personal circumstances, studies, or anything else), talk to a wellbeing adviser.

1. Check whether you live in [university accommodation.](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/managing-student-life/book-an-appointment/university-residences/)
2. Select the right option for you:

## If you live in University accommodation

* [book an online appointment](https://outlook.office365.com/owa/calendar/bk_ResiWellbeing@bristol.ac.uk/bookings/)
* [book an in-person appointment](https://outlook.office365.com/owa/calendar/bk_ResiWellbeing@bristol.ac.uk/bookings/)

## If you live in private accommodation (not university accommodation)

* [book an online appointment](https://outlook.office365.com/owa/calendar/bk_Wellbeing_virtual@bristol.ac.uk/bookings/)
* [book a telephone appointment](https://outlook.office365.com/owa/calendar/bk_Wellbeing_virtual@bristol.ac.uk/bookings/)
* [book an in-person appointment](https://outlook.office365.com/owa/calendar/bk_Wellbeing_inperson@bristol.ac.uk/bookings/)

You can also [call +44 (0)117 456 9860](tel:+441174569860) to book an appointment. This phoneline is staffed 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.

## Get support for sexual violence or misconduct

If you have experienced sexual violence or misconduct, [contact our specialist team](/students/support/health/sexual-violence-support-service/).

## How advisers can help

Book a 45-minute appointment with a wellbeing adviser to discuss any difficulties you are having.

The appointment should help you:

* understand your options and what is available to you
* take control of your situation with confidence.

We can offer support, advice or refer you to other services to help you overcome challenges at the university.

[Our confidentiality statement](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/health/policies/student-services-confidentiality-statement/) explains how we handle and share information.
