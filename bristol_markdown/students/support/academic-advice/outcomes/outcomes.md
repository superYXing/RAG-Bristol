> How to find your results and what they mean for your next stage of study.

## [View your assessment results](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/academic-advice/outcomes/view-assessment-results/)

View your assessment and reassessment (resit) marks, and unit results.

## [What your results mean](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/academic-advice/outcomes/your-results-mean/)

What the information in your results mean.

## [How and when your results are agreed](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/academic-advice/outcomes/how-when-results-agreed/)

How your assessment results and academic outcome are decided. Also, when your assessment results are released.

## [Reassessments](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/academic-advice/outcomes/resits/)

If you cannot progress to the next stage of your studies, the exam board may give you another chance (a resit) to complete assessments for units you have not passed.

## [Supplementary year](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/academic-advice/outcomes/supplementary-year/)

In some cases, you may be offered an extra year of study to help meet progression requirements. Learn about attendance requirements, fees and other things to consider.

## [Conditional progression](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/academic-advice/outcomes/conditional-progression/)

If you fail a unit, you may be able to do a reassessment alongside your next year of study. You must pass your reassessment to continue with your course. Learn about the criteria for conditional progression, fees and attendance.

## [Required to transfer course (Integrated Masters)](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/academic-advice/outcomes/required-to-transfer/)

If you have not met the requirements to progress, you may need to transfer to a different course. Find out what this means for your fees, funding and visa.

## [Required to withdraw from your course](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/academic-advice/outcomes/required-to-withdraw/)

If you have not demonstrated satisfactory progress, you may need to withdraw from your course. Learn about why this might happen, what exit award you may receive and what it means for your visa, accommodation and fees.
