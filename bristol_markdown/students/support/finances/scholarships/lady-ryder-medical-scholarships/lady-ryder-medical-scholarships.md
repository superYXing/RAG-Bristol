> Two five-year living cost awards for Home students who either hold an offer to study on the MB ChB Medicine (A100) course in 2025/26 or are currently on year zero of the MB ChB Gateway to Medicine (A108).

Applications for the 2025/26 academic year have now closed.

Two awards may be available in 2025/26, which would provide successful applicants with a non-repayable living cost award of £7,500 per academic year for five years.

Students who were awarded this scholarship:

* between 2022/23 and 2024/25 will receive £7,500 per academic year for up to five years
* in 2021/22 will receive £7,400 per academic year for up to five years.

This award is funded through the generosity of the [Lady Ryder of Warsaw Memorial Trust](https://www.lrwmt.org.uk/).

## Eligibility

You can apply if you:

* hold an offer to study on the MB ChB Medicine (A100) course in 2025/26 having been awarded a place following a successful interview, or are currently on year zero of the MB ChB Gateway to Medicine (A108);
* are a Home/UK student;
* are a resident of the United Kingdom;
* are eligible for the financial support available from the UK government (in the form of tuition fee and living costs loans from the Student Loans Company);
* have applied for or intend to apply for means-tested support from your regional funding provider (such as Student Finance England) and have consented to share your household income details with the University. You must apply for means-tested support to enable the University to get confirmation of your household income from the Student Loans Company. However, **you are not required to take out any loans you are eligible to receive** if you do not wish to take them.
* are eligible for the financial support available from the UK government (in the form of the tuition fee and living costs loans from the Student Loans Company)
* have a household income of £50,000 or less as confirmed by the Student Loans Company

[Read the full eligibility criteria](/students/support/finances/scholarships/lady-ryder-medical-scholarships/terms/).

## How to apply

Lady Ryder is part of the additional funding programme.

Apply via the [additional funding page](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/scholarships/additional-funding/).
