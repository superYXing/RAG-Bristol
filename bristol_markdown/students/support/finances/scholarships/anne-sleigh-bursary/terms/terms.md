Go to an overview of the [Anne and Peggy Sleigh Bursaries](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/scholarships/anne-sleigh-bursary/).

## Eligibility

To be eligible for the Anne and Peggy Sleigh Bursaries, you must:

* be a female student from the UK;
* be eligible for financial support from your regional funding provider (i.e. Student Finance England, Student Finance Wales, Student Finance Northern Ireland or Student Awards Agency for Scotland);
* be eligible for the financial support available from the UK government in the form of the tuition fee and living costs loans from the Student Loans Company;
* have an offer (or be awaiting a decision) to study an undergraduate programme at the University of Bristol;
* have a demonstrable financial need.

Priority will be given to applicants who:

* are from areas of low participation in higher education;
* have attended an aspiring state school;
* who have received a contextual offer.

## Award

The bursary is valued at £2,500 per year for up to four years of study. It is awarded in the form of a contribution towards living costs.

* Two bursaries are available in 2025/26 for Physics students.
* Two bursaries are available in 2025/26 for Biology students.

You can receive an Anne and Peggy Sleigh Bursary at the same time as the [University of Bristol Bursary](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/scholarships/uob-bursary/ "https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/scholarships/uob-bursary/").

## How to apply

Apply via the [additional funding page](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/scholarships/additional-funding/). When you apply for additional funding, you complete one application form but are considered for several scholarships and bursaries. You can read more about the awards on the [additional funding page](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/scholarships/additional-funding/).

All applications must be received by the published deadline. The online application form will close once the deadline has passed. There is no appeal process and requests to submit late applications will not be considered.

## Other criteria

* The award is available for the duration of your undergraduate course, for up to four years.
* If you need to repeat any years of your course, the award will not be paid for the repeat year of study.
* If you need to suspend your studies due to extenuating circumstances, the remainder of the award will be paid when you restart your studies. No more than £2,500 per academic year will be paid.
* Recipients will be required to provide an annual progress report to the donor via the Development and Alumni Relations Office.
* The bursary will be renewed each year providing you meet the following criteria:
  + You are performing at a 2:1 level or above. Exceptional circumstances will be taken into account where appropriate.
  + Depending upon the subject for which the bursary was originally awarded, you must remain registered within the School of Physics or School of Biology during your course of study.
