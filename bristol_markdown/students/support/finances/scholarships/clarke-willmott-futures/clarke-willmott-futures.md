> One scholarship for a Home UK undergraduate student studying single or joint honours Law.

The bursary is £6,000 overall. You will receive £2,000 in your first, second and third years.

There is also a further £1,000 available as Career Employability funding.

## About Clarke Willmott and Neil Baker

Clarke Willmott is a leading national law firm with offices across areas in England and Wales, including Bristol.

Neil Baker graduated from the University of Bristol in 1989 with a degree in Biochemistry, before continuing his training to become a solicitor. He was a Partner at Clarke Willmott up until 2024, when he sadly passed away. He joined the firm in 1999, becoming a widely respected and leading planning lawyer and a valued member of the firm.

Clarke Willmott have dedicated this scholarship in his memory to support the next generation of lawyers from the University of Bristol.

## Eligibility

You can apply if you are a Home UK undergraduate student with an offer (or awaiting a decision) to study single or joint honours Law at the University of Bristol Law School.

## How to apply

The Clarke Willmott futures scholarship is part of [the additional funding programme](/students/support/finances/scholarships/additional-funding/).

Apply using [the Additional Funding online application form](https://forms.office.com/Pages/ResponsePage.aspx?id=MH_ksn3NTkql2rGM8aQVGyaZAIvbt81KrKBR725vQtBUQjUzN1VPTFk4T0FMR01HOEVEVzk4M00zRi4u).
