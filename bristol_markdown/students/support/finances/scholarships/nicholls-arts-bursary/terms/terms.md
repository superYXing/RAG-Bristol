Go to an overview of [The Nicholls Arts Bursary](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/scholarships/nicholls-arts-bursary/).

## Eligibility

To be eligible for the Nicholls Arts Bursary, you must:

* have an offer (or be awaiting a decision) to study a [single or joint honours Theatre course](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/study/undergraduate/theatre/) in the Department of Theatre or English at the University of Bristol
* not previously have been enrolled on an undergraduate degree programme
* be a Home/UK student
* be eligible for financial support from your regional funding provider (i.e. Student Finance England, Student Finance Wales, Student Finance Northern Ireland, or Student Awards Agency for Scotland)
* have a household income of £50,000 or less.

We will check your household income details with the Student Loans Company. For us to do this, you must have applied for means-tested support from the Student Loans Company and have given your consent (along with your parents/sponsors) to share this information.

## Award

The bursary is valued at £3,000 for the first year of study only and is awarded as a living cost bursary.

Three bursaries are available in 2025/26. You can receive the Nicholls Arts Bursary at the same time as the [University of Bristol Bursary](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/fees-funding/awards/uob-bursary/).

## How to apply

The Nicholls Arts Bursary is part of the additional funding programme. Apply via the [additional funding page](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/scholarships/additional-funding/).

All applications must be received by the published deadline. The online application form will close once the deadline has passed. There is no appeal process and requests to submit late applications will not be considered.

## Other criteria

* The award is only available for the first year of your course.
* If you need to repeat the first year of your course, the award will not be paid for the repeat year of study.
* If, due to exceptional circumstances, you need to suspend your studies during your first year, the remainder of the award will be paid when you restart your studies but no more than £3,000 will be paid.
* Recipients are required to provide a progress report to the donor via the Development and Alumni Relations Office.
