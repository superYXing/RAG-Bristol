> Find out what support is available to help you settle in and make friends.

Your school, university residence, Students' Union, and all our support services are here to help you meet people and feel at home.

## Join in

Find an activity you enjoy, with more than 350 Students' Union [societies, clubs, groups and support networks](https://www.bristolsu.org.uk/student-groups) to choose from.

Make friends from all over the world at the [Global Lounge](http://globallounge.blogs.bristol.ac.uk/). Help yourself to free hot drinks and biscuits, and check out their year-round events programmes, open to all students.

You can [see what's on](https://bristol.ac.uk/students/life-in-bristol/campus/events/) and join in with one of the many events taking place. Try something new and make friends. 

## Religion

Our Multifaith Chaplaincy is a place to meet people from all faiths and religions or none. We can help you [find a place to worship](/multifaith-chaplaincy/places-to-worship/), on campus or in the city.

## Speak to a current student

If you would like to talk to a current student before you arrive, [peer mentoring is available](/students/support/peer-mentoring/ "Peer%20mentors") for first-year undergraduates.

## International postgraduate taught students

Starting life in a new city can be daunting. Our Bristol Voices programme links Bristol-based international postgraduate taught students with an alumni contact to help you make the most of your Bristol experience.

Join the [Bristol Voices programme](/alumni/get-involved/bristol-voices-/) to gain insider knowledge about your new city, practice your language skills, ask questions about life in the UK and Bristol.

## Mature students and part-time students

If you are a [mature or part-time](/students/life-in-bristol/mature/) student, join one of our mingles to meet other mature students and our Mature Student Adviser.
