> What to do when you arrive and advice on how to settle in successfully.

## Confirm your registration details

We recommend you:

* confirm your registration details online by 5 September 2025
* are here in Bristol from 15 September 2025 for Welcome Week
* fully register within Welcome Week.

Find out [how to register](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/new/before-you-arrive/register/).

## Complete your student document check

To complete registration, some students need to present their documents to prove they can study in the UK.

We’ll tell you if this applies to you. Your online student record will also show if you need to complete a document check.

Find out [how to complete your student document check](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/new/before-you-arrive/register/right-to-study-check/).

## Set up your University IT account

Your IT account, including your email, will be created after you confirm your registration details. The first time you sign in, you'll need to set up multi-factor authentication.

Learn about the [IT services available to you](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/it/).

## Download the University of Bristol app

[Download the University of Bristol app](/students/new/university-bristol-app/) to access key student services on the go, including Blackboard, maps, security, and support. You’ll also get useful notifications and use it to record your attendance.

## Find accommodation

Get advice on [finding privately rented accommodation.](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/accommodation/privately-rented/)

## Pay your fees

Find out [how to pay your tuition and accommodation fees](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/pay-your-fees/), including guidance for sponsored students.

You should also [open a student bank account](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/finances/advice/bank-accounts/) if you haven't already.

## Settle into student life

**Go to Destination Bristol**, our event for new students, to:

* do craft activities
* play games
* explore Senate House and nearby University buildings
* learn about our support services
* get a free doughnut.

Join on 15 and 16 September from 9.30 am to 4 pm. No need to book. More information is on the University of Bristol app. [Download the app from Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.ombiel.campusm.bristol&hl=en_GB&gl=US) or the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/university-of-bristol-check-in/id6451236995).

**[Sign up for a Welcome Talk](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/life-in-bristol/campus/events/calendar/#start_date=2025-09-06;end_date=2025-09-21;organizers=677987329)** on Monday 15 September. Learn about the events, groups, support services and facilities you can use as a student.

[Find ways to connect with others](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/new/before-you-arrive/meeting-people-and-support/), from the Global Lounge to student networks, clubs and societies.

Explore [transport options and view our campus map](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/life-in-bristol/maps-and-travel/).

## Take care of your health and wellbeing

[Register with a local doctor](https://www.nhs.uk/nhs-services/gps/how-to-register-with-a-gp-surgery/) so you can access medical care if you need it. Most students register with the [Student Health Service](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students-health/) if they live in the catchment area.

If you're new to the UK:

* [learn how to access medical help](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students-health/international-students/)
* bring a summary of any medical conditions and vaccinations (in English)
* bring a two-month supply of any medication to allow time to transfer your care.

[Let us know if you have a long-term health condition or disability](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/disability-support/tell-us/) so we can support you.

Explore our [wellbeing services and support](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/support/managing-student-life/).
