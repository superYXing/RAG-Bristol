> Develop your study skills and take part in peer-assisted study sessions.

## [Study skills](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/your-studies/study-support/study-skills/)

Study skills services are available to everyone, no matter what course you're on or your level of study.

## [Peer assisted study sessions](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/your-studies/study-support/pass/)

Weekly study sessions led by students from later years of your course, where you can ask questions about your subject area.

## [Library and research skills](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/library/subject-support/libraryskills/)

Equip yourself with the skills and resources to find and use information with integrity in your academic work.

## [Referencing](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/library/subject-support/libraryskills/referencing/)

Referencing is an essential aspect of academic writing. Find out best practices and get help with using referencing software.

## [Independent study](https://www.ole.bris.ac.uk/bbcswebdav/courses/Study_Skills/study-from-home/)

Hints and tips on how to plan and prioritise your workload.

## [Academic language courses](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/academic-language/study/)

Our academic language and literacy courses are designed to help you meet expectations and communicate effectively.

## [Writing fellows](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/bristol-futures/royal-literary-fellows/)

One-on-one consultations to advise you on how to improve your written work.

## [Track your skills](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/life-in-bristol/skills/)

Reflect on your current skills and plan your personal, academic, and career development.
