> Lecture recordings, reading lists and information on how you will be assessed.

## [Blackboard](https://www.ole.bris.ac.uk)

Blackboard is our online learning environment where you can access course activities and materials.

## [Watch a lecture](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/digital-education/tools/replay/student-essentials/)

Find out how to use Re/Play to watch a recording of a lecture. These typically become available two days after the lecture has taken place.

## [How we teach](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/your-studies/teaching-and-learning/how-we-teach/)

Your course will include a combination of lectures, seminars, tutorials, practicals and peer-to-peer learning.

## [Assessments](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/your-studies/teaching-and-learning/assessments/)

The different types of assessment you experience will vary depending on your course.

## [Feedback](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/your-studies/teaching-and-learning/feedback/)

Use the feedback you receive on your assessments to help your personal learning and development.

## [Reading lists](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/library/use/resource-lists/students/)

Find books and access online resources that have been recommended by your tutors and lecturers.

## [Bristol Futures](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/bristol-futures/)

Tackle the key challenges in global citizenship, sustainable futures and innovation and enterprise.

## [Unit feedback surveys](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/your-studies/teaching-and-learning/unit-feedback-surveys/)

Provide us with feedback for each of your units so we can continue to evolve and enhance our teaching.

## [Registering attendance](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/students/your-studies/teaching-and-learning/registering-attendance/)

Learn about registering your attendance at on-campus teaching using the University of Bristol app.
