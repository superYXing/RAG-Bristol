## Students from a lower socioeconomic group

You may self-identify as coming from a lower socioeconomic group because you:

* live in an [Indices of Multiple Deprivation (IMD) quintile 1 area](https://www.bristol.ac.uk/study/undergraduate/entry-requirements-qualifications/contextual-offers/#:~:text=school%20or%20college-,You%20live%20in%20an%20Indices%20of%20Multiple%20Deprivation%20(IMD)%20quintile%201%20or%202%20area)
* have been eligible for free school meals
* are eligible for [the University of Bristol Bursary](/students/support/finances/scholarships/uob-bursary/).

This is not a complete list.

## Our Explore community

Our Explore community can help you make the most of the support available. It runs events and activities for students from lower socioeconomic groups throughout the year, so you can:

* meet other students and share experiences
* understand what support is available
* settle in and make the most out of your time here.

If you have any questions about Explore, email [explore-uob@bristol.ac.uk](mailto:explore-uob@bristol.ac.uk).

## Get support

Our specialised [support teams](/students/support/) are here to help you throughout your time at university.

### Financial support

If you're experiencing financial hardship, our [University hardship funds](/students/support/finances/hardship/) may be able to help.

You may also be eligible for our [scholarships, awards and bursaries](/students/support/finances/scholarships/).

### Academic support

You will have support throughout your course from someone in your department, such as a [personal tutor](/students/support/personal-tutors/).

[Student-led study sessions](/students/your-studies/study-support/pass/) and [study support](/students/your-studies/study-support/) such as resources, workshops and drop-ins are also available.

### Peer support

If you are [starting in September](/students/new/), you can request a [peer mentor](/students/support/peer-mentoring/) to help you in the first term.
